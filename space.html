Galaxies Unknown

In the event of moon disaster speech, william safire. irrigation, unscientifically conducted, would not give us such truly wonderful mathematical fitness [as we observe in the martian canals]... it is surrounded by a light blue aureole that gradually darkens, becoming turquiose, dark blue, violet, and finally coal black. it is the speed alone that sustains him. our spiritual need for an open frontier. our sun is one of 100 billion stars in our galaxy. people will soon get to experience the moon in ways they never imagined. telescopes are in some ways like time machines. the first day or so we all pointed to our countries. the single overarching goal of human space flight is the human settlement of the solar system, and eventually beyond. there are innumerable worlds of different sizes. there was no grand musical accompaniment; no triumphant, inspired sonata or symphony. there's the whole myth about rocket science. we are here for the beauty. we fooled ourselves into thinking this thing wouldn't crash. why, i ask myself, shouldn't the shining dots of the sky be as accessible as the black dots on the map of france?.

Is this the end of the story?
